IoT based Autonomous Tunnel Electrical Cable Fault Detection and Maintenance Robot

The system presents a model of a robot that can inspect the surrounding conditions of the underground tunnel by traveling from one end of the tunnel to another without any human interference. With the help of the sensors employed on the Robot, all the information about underground tunnels such as the presence of harmful gases, temperature, obstacles, and fire accidents is transmitted by a wireless means through RF transceiver. Along with this data, the Robot detects physical damage on the wire and sends fault location to the receiving station through RF transceiver. Hence the above data helps the user to estimate the safety or danger level and accordingly to decide the plan of action to solve the problem in the fastest way possible.

Enhanced Architecture for high resolution in Digital Potentiometers

This approach uses four digital potentiometers connected in such a fashion that they can be configured as a series connection, parallel connection or a combination of both digitally. It consists of a Configuration ROM containing data about all the possible configurations. The minimum and maximum resistance offered by a single 4 bit Digital potentiometer is 1kΩ and 16kΩ respectively, this architecture allows us to gain a minimum and maximum of 0.25kΩ and 64kΩ respectively.

Digital Land Ownership based on Geofencing of Rural areas and Crop rotation management for Efficient Farming Practices

In this project, we made an IoT based module to record weather data and GPS data of 4 distinct corner points of a farm and were placed as far from each other as possible. Using that data, accurate geofencing of farms was done. After web-scraping the soil mineral content data of those co-ordinates from publicly available government data, we took the current or previously grown crop as input to suggest the farmer what kind of crop should be grown next that would suit both the soil type and weather. I worked on the Internet of Things part of the project and designed a data logging system for recording the GPS coordinates, overall condition of the data logger, and the weather data. Statistical methods like median were used to improve accuracy and also algorithm was such that minimum power is used and redundant data is not logged. Data was transferred to a Firebase real-time database remotely.

Photonic Crystal Fiber based Refractive Index Sensor Design

In this project, we developed a photonic crystal fiber based refractive index sensor and used it to determine if a cell is a cancer cell or a normal cell. Simulation was implemented by FEM (Finite Element Method) using COMSOL Multiphysics 5.3a. Electromagnetic Waves, Frequency Domain was used and Mode analysis computation results were observed and conclusions were drawn after study of Electric field confinement and distribution and confinement loss peak shift with Basal Cell, Breast Cell, Cervical Cell, Jurkat Cell in the cell cavity. Successfully did enhancements in the electric field confinement of the sensor.
